Use this only after you have a pilot proof artifact you can point to.

## 1. Short Email Version

Subject options:
- `Bounded pilot proof for donor proposal workflows`
- `Pilot evidence: faster reviewable drafts in donor workflows`
- `Proposal operations pilot results (UN/EU strongest proof)`

Body:

```text
Hi [Name],

I’m reaching out because I think GrantFlow may be relevant to teams managing complex institutional proposal workflows.

GrantFlow is not a grant-writing chatbot. It is a proposal operations platform designed to help teams move from a raw concept to a structured, reviewable draft package with workflow control, review traceability, and export-ready outputs.

We recently completed a bounded NGO pilot across three donor tracks: UN agencies, EU, and USAID. The strongest proof came from the UN and EU paths, where the team moved faster to reviewable drafts, reduced review loops, and reduced review noise. The USAID path also improved, but remained more coordination-heavy and is best described with conditions rather than as fully solved.

If useful, I can send a short external proof summary and set up a 45–60 minute conversation to see whether the same operating problems exist in your proposal workflow.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## 5. What To Say If They Ask For Proof

Use this wording:
- `We have strongest pilot proof on UN and EU proposal workflows.`
- `The USAID path also improved materially, but we describe it with conditions because coordination load remained higher.`
- `So the honest claim is not that every donor path is equally mature; the honest claim is that the proposal operations model is working, with strongest evidence on UN/EU and conditional proof on USAID.`

## 6. What Not To Claim

Do not say:
- `It fully automates donor proposal writing.`
- `It eliminates review loops.`
- `It works equally well across all donor paths.`
- `It replaces human compliance review.`
